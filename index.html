Nutrition

Nutrition is process by which organisms obtain, consume and utilise food to maintain life processes

Some organisms obtain already made food from others while some manufacture their own .After consuming the food, it is utilised in different ways in the body.

Modes of nutrition

There are two modes of nutrition namely;

  1. Autotrophic nutrition
  2. Heterotrophic nutrition

Autotrophic nutrition

This is where organisms manufacture or synthesize their own food using a specific form of energy.Organisms that make their own food are called autotrophs.

Autotrophs are divided into two groups according to the energy used when makng their own food ie;

  • Photosynthesis
  • Chemosynthesis

Photosynthesis

Photosynthesis is the process by which organisms make their own food using sunlight energy. Organisms that make their food by photosynthesis and called phototrophs.examples include;

  • greenplant
  • algae
  • photosynthetic bacteria

Chemosynthesis

This is a process whereby organisms manufacture organic food materials using energy from specific chemical reactions known as oxidation reactions. Organisms that carryout chemosynthesis are called chemotrophs and examples in include;

  • Nitrosomonas
  • Sulphur colorless bacteria
  • Nitrococcus

NB: Oxidation reactions are exothermic reactions which means they give out heat as a product.

NH4(aq)+O2(g) -> NO2(aq)+H2O(l)+ heat

Heterotrophic nutrition

This is a mode of nutrition whose organisms obtain already manufactured food from other organisms hence they can't make their own food.And they can be divided into;

  • Parasitism
  • Mutualism
  • Phagocytosis nutrition
  • Saprophytism nutrition
  • Holozoic nutrition

Parasitism

This is a form of nutrition relationships between organisms of different species where one organism(parasite) benefits nutritionally and the other (host) suffers harm eg.

  • ticks in cows
  • tapeworms in a cow,pigs and humans
  • liverflukes in a sheep
  • mosquitoes on human beings
  • caterpillar on leaves of plants

Mutualism

This is a nutritional relationship between organisms of different species where both organisms benefit from from the relationship eg. Lichen, a relationship between an algae and fungi.

Phagocytosis

This is a form of nutrition where the organism engulfs food particles. This is common in unicellular organisms like paramecium,amoeba etc. During phagocytosis, intracellular digestion takesplace ie it happens inside cells.

Saprophytism

This is a form of nutrition where organisms feed on decomposing or decaying matter.Examples include bacteria,moulds,mushrooms.

Holozoic nutrition

This is a form of nutrition where the organisms takes in food, break it down into simpler substances which are absorbed and utilised the body. Organisms that undergo holozoic nutrition are divided into three;

  • Carnivores

These are organisms that only feed on flesh.

  • Herbivores

There are organisms that feed entirely on vegetation eg. goats,cows,sheep.

  • Omnivores

These are organisms that feed on both flesh and vegetation.examples include; man, pigs, rats etc.

NB: Important terms used in nutrition

  1. Ingestion; This refers to the taking in of food int the body.
  2. Digestion; This is the breakdown of food into simpler substances that can be absorbed in the body.
  3. Absorption; This is the process where digested food is taken into the cells of the body.
  4. Assimilation; This utilizing of the absobed food where is is turned into energy.

Classes of nutrition.

Under classes of nutrition, we have types of food and food is any substance that can be digested and utilised by the body to maintain life processes.

Importance of food

  1. To provide the body with energy through respiration.
  2. Boosting the body's immunity.
  3. Growth and building of new cells.
  4. Reparing of damaged tissues and replacing of dead ones.

Types of food

There are seven different types of food classes eg.

  • Carbohydrates

    • These are energy giving foods and examples include;

    • cassava
    • rice
    • wheat

  • Vitamins

    • These are foods that boost bodies immunity eg.

    • Vitamin K from green vegetables like spinach, cabbage.
    • Vitamin D from egg yolk, margarine and action of sunlight on skin.
    • Vitamin A from fish,liver oil,carrots.
  • Proteins

    These are body building foods and examples include;

    • beans
    • beef
    • chicken
  • Mineral salts

    • These are also immunity boosting foods. They are also required in small quantities for normal body growth.Examples include;

    • Calcium: This helps in formation of strong bones and teeth.
    • Iron: Formation of haemoglobin in red blood cells.
    • Chlorine: Formation of hydrochloric acid in gastric juice.

  • Roughages

    • This is indigestible cellulose from plant cell walls. They stimulate peristalysis in the intestines.

  • Lipids(fats and oils)

    • These works as energy reserves in animals,seeds and fruits. Examples include meat, groundnuts.

  • Water

    • Without water, life couldn't exist on Earth. It is used in a lot of processes which include

    • photosynthesis
    • Translocation
    • osmoregulation etc.
